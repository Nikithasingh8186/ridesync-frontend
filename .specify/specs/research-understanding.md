# Research and Understanding

## Problem Space

### The Core Problem
Employees commuting to the same office take separate cabs
despite living in the same zones, wasting money and adding
to traffic and carbon emissions.

### Who Feels This Pain
- Individual employees paying Rs 300-500 per day on solo cabs
- Companies with no visibility into commute waste
- City infrastructure absorbing unnecessary traffic

### Market Validation
- Average Hyderabad cab fare: Rs 250-400 one way
- 2 people sharing = Rs 125-200 each = Rs 100-200 saved per trip
- 22 working days/month = Rs 2,200-4,400 saved per employee/month
- CO2: avg cab emits 120g CO2/km; sharing halves per-person impact

## User Research

### User Type 1: The Rider
- Wants to save money
- Does not want to wait long
- Needs to trust the driver (coworker = trusted)
- Pain: no easy way to find who lives nearby

### User Type 2: The Driver
- Already driving, wants to fill empty seats
- Wants minimal coordination overhead
- Pain: no platform to broadcast their route

### User Type 3: HR / Admin
- Wants to show sustainability metrics
- Wants to reduce transport allowance costs
- Pain: no data on employee commute patterns

## Competitive Landscape

Product     | Gap
------------|--------------------------------------------------
BlaBlaCar   | Public, not company-internal, trust issue
Uber Pool   | Not employee-specific, no recurring rides
WhatsApp    | Manual, no matching, no history
RideSync    | Internal, trusted, smart matching, tracked savings

## Key Insights
1. Trust is solved by making it internal — coworkers only
2. The matching problem is zone + time, not exact address
3. Recurring rides are the real value — same people daily
4. Savings visualization drives adoption more than convenience
